1
Support by purchasing books with diversity and diverse authors
Sales speak volumes so if you purchase diverse books, it tells the publishers that there is a demand for them. If the author has a good sale on their first book they are more likely to sell the second one and get a higher advance.





2
Show up in a local library and during literature events
If you can’t purchase the books, you can go to you library and look for diverse books to read. Also you ask around for recommendations and talk to your librarian about it.





3
Use your influence on social media and in real life
Show your support towards diverse book and authors on both book-specific (like Goodreads) and non-book-specific social media (Instagram, TikTok). Also, social media is a great way for people to discover books!





4
Create your own resources and support marginalized creators
Support blogs about diverse books or even create your own! By blogging about diverse books specifically, it makes it easier for people to find them. Also, by giving marginalized authors the attention and support, you help them find the resources they need to succeed.





5
Speak up when the books don’t get it right
If you read a book where you feel the representation is off, search it up on Google. The chances are, other people are talking about it too. You can also email the publishers directly.





6
Donate your time or money to organizations
Help by donating money or volunteering at organization like We Need Diverse Books to support their initiatives. There are also other events, organizations and book awards you can support.





7
Read for fun
Don’t read diverse books because you think you should. Reading them should not be seen as spinach. Read whatever genre you like! The book doesn’t have to be necessarily focused on diversity. It could be focused on something else and the characters just happen to be diverse.
